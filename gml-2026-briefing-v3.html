Preface How to read this

The keynote moved fast and the language was confident, but almost nothing announced has shipped. Most of it is beta, pilot, or "coming this summer." A briefing written today is not a roadmap — it's a reading list with a few small experiments attached.

Each section that follows has three parts: the announcement, a sharper read that pushes back on Google's narrative where the math or the history warrants it, and one pilot structured as a real experiment — hypothesis, success criteria, cost, revert condition. Treat the sharp reads as opinions, not facts. Treat the pilots as invitations.

i.
Search & AI ModeAd becomes answer

The ad becomes the answer.

Inside AI Mode, Google is composing ad creative in real time to respond to the user's question and the AI-written answer above it. Eligibility is gated to AI Max for Search or PMax with text customisation.

  • a
    Conversational Discovery Ads Gemini-tailored creative answering the user's exact question. Labelled "Sponsored."
  • b
    Highlighted Answers When AI Mode lists recommendations, eligible ads appear inline as one of them.
  • c
    AI-Powered Shopping Ads Gemini picks SKUs and writes a per-query explainer for why your product fits.
  • d
    Business Agent for Leads A brand-tuned chat agent inside the ad. Testing in education, auto, real estate.
Sharper Read
Google is selling reach, but the reach is unproven. AI Mode has 1B monthly users — we have no public data on what share of that traffic carries genuine purchase intent. We've watched this exact pattern with AMP, Discover, and Local Service Ads: early-adopter bonus, then dilution. The structural story matters more than the surface story: any account still on manual keywords is being quietly cut out of an emerging surface whose actual revenue contribution we cannot yet measure.
Pilot Proposal N° 01
Hypothesis
AI Max surfaces incremental long-tail queries our keyword structure misses, without degrading CPA on existing terms.
Success
≥30% of conversions from queries not present in baseline keyword set, and blended CPA within 15% of baseline.
Cost
~20% of Search budget on one account, 30 days. Low client trust risk if disclosed as a learning pilot.
Revert
Either threshold missed by day 21. Document why and add to the team's internal AI Max playbook.
ii.
AI BriefA new craft layer

A brief for Gemini.

Three layers of plain-English input — Messaging, Matching, Audience — that steer how AI Max writes copy and routes queries. If it ships as described, we can A/B test brand propositions inside one campaign.

  • a
    Messaging Guidelines Voice, regulated language, claim rules. What ads should and shouldn't say.
  • b
    Matching Guidelines Queries to capture and deflect. The closest thing to keyword strategy left.
  • c
    Audience Guidelines Who to reach, and how to talk to them. Targeting and creative tailoring in one input.
Sharper Read
This is the only genuine strategic upgrade in the whole keynote — but it introduces a hidden risk. Today, when creative underperforms, we can isolate copy from targeting from match types. With AI Brief, one bad input contaminates the whole campaign and we lose the ability to diagnose by component. We'll need a new debugging methodology — probably treating the brief itself as a versioned artifact with rollback. Nobody is talking about this yet.
Pilot Proposal N° 02
Hypothesis
We can articulate a client's positioning clearly enough that a colleague unfamiliar with the brand could produce on-brand ad copy from the brief alone.
Success
Peer test: show the brief (not the brand) to a colleague. They write 5 ads. If ≥3 pass brand QA, the brief works.
Cost
~2 hours of strategy time. Zero spend. Pure internal R&D.
Revert
N/A — even a failed brief tells us where our positioning thinking is weak.
iii.
Ask AdvisorOne agent, four products

One agent across the stack.

A unified Gemini agent spanning Ads, Analytics, Merchant Center, and GMP. Ask it to "find new customers for my hair care products" and it's meant to pull feed data, audience signals, and draft a campaign in one flow. English-only beta.

Sharper Read
Google has tried to ship cross-product assistance for three years and the Ads Advisor and Analytics Advisor we have today are mediocre at best. The bet here is that Gemini 3 finally makes the agent good. Possible, but unproven. This is the section to be most skeptical about. Cost of being wrong on it: low. Cost of being right and not testing it: medium.
Pilot Proposal N° 03
Hypothesis
The agent can complete a multi-step task (e.g. "find underperforming Shopping keywords across all campaigns and propose negatives") without us re-prompting more than twice.
Success
≤2 corrections needed. Output actionable without manual rework.
Cost
~30 minutes of one analyst's time per task. No client exposure.
Revert
3+ corrections needed across two tasks. Document failure modes; revisit in 90 days.

Demand GenYouTube as performance

YouTube wants to be performance.

Demand Gen — particularly with product feeds and creator partnerships — is being positioned as a peer to PMax and Search rather than an awareness layer.

  • a
    Demand Gen on Google Maps Promoted Pins for foot-traffic plays. Relevant for brick-and-mortar.
  • b
    Product feeds expand Tablets, YouTube Pause ads, and new verticals including automotive.
  • c
    Creator partnerships in setup Eligible creators surface inside Google Ads. Organic videos convert to paid assets.

Sharper Read
The +33% number is almost certainly cannibalized from PMax and Search, not net-new conversions. Google has run this exact framing before — same lift shape, same selective stat — and our own data on PMax expansion never quite matched the keynote slides. Measure incrementality, not attributed lift. The genuine unlock in this section is creator partnerships in setup, not the product feed extension itself.
Pilot Proposal N° 04
Hypothesis
DG with product feeds drives net-new conversions for a DTC client, not cannibalized PMax/Search volume.
Success
Geo-incrementality test: ≥8% total conversion lift in test geos vs. holdout. (Below Google's claim — we're measuring real, not attributed.)
Cost
4-week test, ~15% of budget allocated to test geos. One DTC client with a real catalogue.
Revert
<5% incrementality at day 28. Likely cannibalization confirmed; reallocate budget.
v.
Asset StudioVolume over polish

Creative on a real model stack.

Asset Studio is being repositioned as the creative hub inside Google Ads with Gemini 3 Pro, Imagen 4, and Veo 3 underneath. Gemini Omni Flash this summer. Included with Ads at no extra cost; Veo 3 advanced features carry a spend-linked quota.

Sharper Read
Generated creative will be median-quality at best for another eighteen months. Veo 3 and Imagen 4 are impressive in demos and inconsistent in production — we've seen this with every previous generation. The real interesting move here isn't quality, it's volume. Being able to run fifty variants of a creative hypothesis cheaply changes what we can learn, even if no individual asset is great. That's the angle worth testing.
Pilot Proposal N° 05
Hypothesis
Asset Studio produces a usable creative variant in under 10 minutes that would have taken our creative team more than 2 hours.
Success
≥1 in 5 generated assets passes brand QA without major edits. Time-to-asset under 15 minutes.
Cost
~4 hours of testing time with one well-documented brand. Zero media spend.
Revert
All assets need substantial human cleanup. Treat as a 2027 conversation.
vi.
Commerce & UCPGoogle as cash register

Google as the transactional layer.

Universal Cart and the Universal Commerce Protocol are the most structural announcements of the keynote — Google positioning itself between the consumer and the merchant at the checkout itself, not just at discovery.

  • a
    Universal Cart Cross-merchant cart. Nike, Sephora, Target, Walmart, Wayfair + Shopify merchants. Live in US.
  • b
    BNPL inside Google Pay Affirm and Klarna integrated at checkout.
  • c
    Merchant Center upgrades AI Performance Insights and Conversational Attributes for AI surface visibility.
Sharper Read
The story Google isn't telling: UCP is the slow death of branded paid search for any retailer that joins it. If a Wayfair shopper checks out inside Google, Wayfair no longer needs to defend its own brand terms on Search — the funnel was never on Wayfair's site to defend. Watch what happens to retailer brand-spend in the back half of the year. For our ecom book, this could quietly redirect 20–30% of branded budget elsewhere within twelve months.
Pilot Proposal N° 06
Hypothesis
Adding Conversational Attributes to one ecom client's feed improves AI surface visibility, regardless of UCP eligibility.
Success
≥10% lift in impression share on Shopping campaigns within 30 days post-implementation. Weekly tracking.
Cost
~6 hours of feed work. Zero spend, zero risk.
Revert
No measurable change at day 45. Document findings; pause further investment.
vii.
MeasurementPredictive signal

From looking back to looking forward.

Measurement shifted from completed-conversion attribution to predictive signal. Three releases here. One — Qualified Future Conversions — matters more than the rest.

  • a
    Qualified Future Conversions (QFCs) A new metric linking current spend to predicted future sales using brand-search-uplift signals. Restricted pilot.
  • b
    Meridian inside Analytics 360 Google's open-source MMM, natively embedded.
  • c
    Meridian GeoX Open-source geo-incrementality testing; signals feed back into the MMM.
Sharper Read
Predictive metrics need ground truth to be trusted. QFCs won't have a clean validation cycle for at least eighteen months of real spend data, and Google has been quiet about the model's confidence intervals. Treat QFCs as directional signal — not a metric you'd defend a budget on yet. The more trustworthy near-term play is Meridian inside GA360. Less novel, more proven, easier to operationalize.
Pilot Proposal N° 07
Hypothesis
Understanding QFC methodology now equips us to take a defensible position when clients ask about it in 2027 budget conversations.
Success
One team member produces a 1-page internal explainer: how the model works, known limitations, when to trust it, when not to.
Cost
3–4 hours of reading and writing time. No client work involved.
Revert
N/A — pure preparation.
viii.
BiddingPacing & journeys

Pacing, finally automated.

Three additions to the bidding toolkit. The pacing one solves a real operational problem on every seasonal account we run.

  • a
    Demand-Led Pacing Search and Shopping. Adjusts daily spend to demand signals while honouring monthly caps.
  • b
    Journey-Aware Bidding (beta) For multi-step lead-gen funnels. Bids on the full path, not just the form-fill.
  • c
    Smart Bidding Exploration → Shopping & PMax Adjustable Target ROAS tolerance to capture incremental traffic.
Sharper Read
Demand-Led Pacing is good, but it's solving for a problem that well-tuned tROAS or tCPA accounts shouldn't have in the first place. The genuinely significant release is Journey-Aware Bidding — if it works, it's the first time Google has bid on something other than the last-click conversion event. That matters for every lead-gen account where revenue happens weeks after the click.
Pilot Proposal N° 08
Hypothesis
Demand-Led Pacing on a promotional account outperforms manual budget management during the peak demand window of a sale event.
Success
≥10% more conversions delivered in peak window for same total spend vs. our manual pacing baseline.
Cost
One promotional campaign, one sale event. Manageable risk.
Revert
Performance below manual baseline at day 7 of sale period. Switch back manually.

Final Read One thought

The honest reading of GML 2026: a great deal was announced, very little has shipped, and the parts that will matter most are not the parts Google chose to spotlight. The story Google is telling is one of AI everywhere. The story underneath is structural — about who controls the surface, who owns the cart, and which surfaces a manual account is being quietly locked out of.

The work over the next ninety days is curiosity, measured. Pick two or three pilots from the list, run them properly with the success criteria documented up front, and let the rest mature. We will learn more from a single well-designed experiment than from chasing every feature at half-speed.
